01 / OBSERVATION

Twelve years of watching
an industry talk to itself.

Crypto content has a target audience problem. It's made by enthusiasts, for enthusiasts, about enthusiasm. The feedback loop is airtight.

The charts are for people who already read charts. The podcasts are for people who already have wallets. The memes require a glossary that nobody publishes because admitting you need one means you're not in the club.

Meanwhile, an industry worth trillions remains functionally invisible to most households. Not because they're opposed. Because nobody has made it worth their attention.

02 / THESIS

Nobody buys Bitcoin because
of a candlestick chart.

They buy it because someone they trust told them it changed their life. Because they watched their neighbor retire at 40. Because their kid asked about it at dinner and they didn't have an answer.

Proof of Lifestyle starts where every other crypto marketing strategy stops: with the people who aren't paying attention yet.

The money is the mechanism. The life is the point.
